Sweet Orange Cultivation Guide

Sweet Orange (Citrus sinensis), also known as Mosambi, is one of the most popular citrus fruits. It grows well in subtropical to tropical climates, requiring warm temperatures, deep soils, and adequate moisture. With proper management, sweet orange orchards can yield high-quality, juicy fruits rich in Vitamin C.

Climate Requirements

Temperature: Ideal range is 20–32°C. Trees cannot withstand frost; prolonged cold damages fruit and flowers.

Rainfall: Requires 1000–1250 mm annual rainfall, well distributed, or supplemental irrigation in dry areas.

Humidity: Moderate humidity (50–70%) is suitable. High humidity may promote fungal diseases.

Soil Requirements

Sweet orange grows best in well-drained loamy soils rich in organic matter. Soil should be slightly acidic to neutral (pH 5.5–7.5). Avoid saline or waterlogged soils.

Watering

Regular watering is essential, especially during flowering and fruiting. Provide irrigation once every 7–10 days in dry seasons. Avoid waterlogging as it can damage roots. Drip irrigation is ideal for uniform moisture and water conservation.

Harvesting

Sweet orange trees start bearing fruits after 3–5 years. Fruits are ready for harvest 7–8 months after flowering, when they attain full color and sweetness. Harvesting is done manually by clipping fruits with stalks to avoid damage.
