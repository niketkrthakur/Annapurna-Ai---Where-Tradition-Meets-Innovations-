Mango Cultivation Guide

Mango grows best in a tropical to subtropical climate with warm temperatures between Daytime 24–30°C (75–86°F), Nighttime 18–22°C (64–72°F). It requires a distinct dry season for flowering and fruit setting. Mango trees thrive in well-draining loamy or alluvial soil with a slightly acidic to neutral pH of 5.5–7.5. Young trees need regular watering, while mature trees are drought-tolerant but sensitive to waterlogging. Proper moisture balance is crucial for high fruit yield and quality.

Climate and Light

Temperature: Ideal range is 24–30°C (75–86°F). Temperatures below 10°C (50°F) may damage flowers and young fruit; above 40°C (104°F) may cause flower/fruit drop.

Photoperiod (Daylight): Mango requires 8–10 hours of full sunlight daily. Shaded areas reduce flowering and yield.

Humidity: Moderate humidity (50–60%) is best. High humidity during flowering can encourage powdery mildew and anthracnose.

Soil Requirements

Mango trees prefer deep, well-draining loamy, alluvial, or sandy loam soils. Soil pH should be maintained between 5.5 and 7.5. Heavy clay soils with poor drainage should be avoided to prevent root rot. Enrich soil with organic matter before planting for better establishment.

Watering

Mango requires moderate but well-managed watering. Young trees need regular irrigation (every 7–10 days) for healthy growth. Mature trees should be watered during dry spells, but irrigation should be reduced or stopped during flowering to encourage fruit set. Overwatering or waterlogging must be avoided as it leads to root rot and poor fruit quality.
