Fresh Apple Orchards

Apple Cultivation Guide

Apple thrives in a temperate to subtropical climate with cold winters and moderate summers. Optimal temperatures are Daytime 21–27°C (70–81°F), Night time 6–15°C (43–59°F). Chilling hours (below 7°C or 45°F) are required in winter for proper flowering and fruit set. Apples prefer well-draining loamy soil with a slightly acidic to neutral pH of 5.5–6.8. Regular irrigation is important during dry spells, though waterlogging must be avoided to protect roots.

Climate and Light

Temperature: Ideal range 21–27°C (70–81°F). Requires 800–1,500 chilling hours below 7°C (45°F) depending on variety. High heat during flowering reduces fruit set.

Photoperiod (Daylight): Requires 6–8 hours of full sunlight daily for healthy growth and fruit color development.

Humidity: Moderate humidity (50–70%) is best. Very high humidity can cause fungal diseases like apple scab and powdery mildew.

Soil Requirements

Apples grow best in deep, well-draining loamy soils rich in organic matter. Maintain soil pH between 5.5 and 6.8. Avoid heavy clay soils with poor aeration and drainage. Organic compost or farmyard manure should be added before planting for healthy establishment.

Watering

Apple trees need moderate but regular watering, especially during fruit development. Young trees require watering every 7–10 days in dry conditions. Mature trees should be irrigated at critical stages: flowering, fruit set, and fruit enlargement. Overwatering and waterlogging must be avoided to prevent root diseases. Drip irrigation is highly recommended.
