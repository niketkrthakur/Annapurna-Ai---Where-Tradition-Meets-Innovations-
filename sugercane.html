Sugarcane Cultivation Guide

Sugarcane is a tropical and subtropical crop mainly grown for sugar and jaggery production. It is a long-duration crop (10–18 months depending on region) and requires warm, humid climate and fertile soils for optimum yield.

Climate Requirements

Temperature: Ideal range is 20–35°C. Growth slows below 15°C and stops below 10°C.

Rainfall: Requires about 100–150 cm annual rainfall or irrigation. Even distribution is important.

Sunlight: At least 7–8 hours daily is needed for photosynthesis and sugar accumulation.

Soil Requirements

Sugarcane thrives in deep, fertile, well-drained loamy soils rich in organic matter. Soil pH should be between 6.0 and 7.5. Heavy clay or waterlogged soils reduce yield, while sandy soils require high irrigation and fertilizers.

Watering

Sugarcane is a water-intensive crop requiring 1500–2500 mm of water over the growing period. Irrigation frequency: every 7–10 days in summer and 15–20 days in winter. Drip irrigation improves water-use efficiency and sugar recovery.

Harvesting

Harvesting is done when canes are mature (10–18 months), with brix (sugar content) at its peak. Signs of maturity: dried lower leaves, metallic sound when tapped, and maximum juice sweetness. Harvesting at the right stage ensures higher sugar yield.
