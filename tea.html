Tea Cultivation Guide

Tea (Camellia sinensis) is one of the most important plantation crops grown mainly in hilly and high-rainfall regions. It requires a humid tropical to subtropical climate, well-drained acidic soils, and frequent rainfall. With proper management, tea bushes can remain productive for decades.

Climate Requirements

Temperature: Optimal range is 18–30°C. Growth slows below 13°C and suffers above 35°C.

Rainfall: Requires 1500–2500 mm of well-distributed annual rainfall.

Humidity: High humidity (70–90%) is favorable for lush leaf growth.

Altitude: Best grown at elevations 600–2000 meters above sea level for high-quality tea.

Soil Requirements

Tea prefers deep, well-drained loamy soils rich in organic matter. Soil should be slightly acidic (pH 4.5–5.5). Avoid alkaline, waterlogged, or compacted soils.

Watering

Tea requires frequent and uniform soil moisture. Supplemental irrigation during dry periods is essential, especially in plains. Drip or sprinkler irrigation helps maintain humidity and ensures uniform growth.

Harvesting

Tea is harvested by plucking the top two leaves and a bud, usually every 7–15 days depending on growth. Plucking continues throughout the growing season, yielding fresh shoots for processing into green, black, or oolong tea.
