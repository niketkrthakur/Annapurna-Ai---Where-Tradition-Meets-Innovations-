Potato Cultivation Guide

Potatoes grow best in a cool-temperate climate with temperatures between Daytime 18–22°C (65–72°F), Nighttime 12–18°C (54–65°F). They need long days (12–14 hours) of daylight for tuber formation. Potatoes thrive in well-draining sandy loam or loam soil with a slightly acidic pH of 5.0–6.5, enriched with compost or well-rotted manure. Adequate and consistent moisture is crucial since potatoes have shallow roots, but overwatering must be avoided to prevent tuber rot.

Climate and Light

Temperature: Ideal range 18–22°C (65–72°F). Growth slows below 10°C (50°F) and above 30°C (86°F).

Photoperiod (Daylight): Long-day crop; requires 12–14 hours of daylight for good tuberization.

Humidity: Moderate humidity around 60–70% is best. High humidity may cause late blight and other fungal diseases.

Soil Requirements

Potatoes prefer light, well-draining sandy loam or loam soil with high organic matter. Maintain soil pH between 5.0 and 6.5. Avoid heavy clay soil that retains too much water. Add compost or aged manure 2–3 weeks before planting for best results.

Watering

Potatoes require consistent but moderate moisture. They need about 25–50 mm of water per week depending on climate. Avoid waterlogging to prevent tuber rot and fungal diseases. Drip irrigation or furrow irrigation is recommended for uniform water distribution.
