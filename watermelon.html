Watermelon Cultivation Guide

Watermelon grows best in a warm, sunny climate with temperatures between Daytime 22–28°C (72–82°F), Nighttime 18–21°C (64–70°F). It is a warm-season crop and highly sensitive to frost. Watermelons thrive in well-draining sandy loam or loam soil with a slightly acidic to neutral pH of 6.0–7.5. They require plenty of sunlight and consistent watering during fruit development, but excess moisture or waterlogging should be avoided to prevent diseases.

Climate and Light

Temperature: Optimum range 22–28°C (72–82°F). Growth slows below 18°C (64°F) and stops below 10°C (50°F). High heat above 35°C (95°F) may reduce fruit set.

Photoperiod (Daylight): Requires 8–10 hours of direct sunlight daily for healthy vine growth and fruit sweetness.

Humidity: Moderate humidity around 50–60% is ideal. High humidity encourages fungal diseases like powdery mildew and anthracnose.

Soil Requirements

Watermelons prefer light, sandy loam or loam soil with good drainage and high organic matter. Maintain soil pH between 6.0 and 7.5. Avoid heavy clay soils as they retain excess moisture and hinder root development. Add compost or well-rotted manure before planting for better fruit quality.

Watering

Watermelon requires consistent moisture during vine and fruit development. Irrigate deeply once every 5–7 days depending on climate. Reduce watering when fruits start maturing to enhance sweetness and prevent cracking. Avoid waterlogging, as excess moisture can cause root rot and reduce fruit quality. Drip irrigation is recommended for efficient water use.
