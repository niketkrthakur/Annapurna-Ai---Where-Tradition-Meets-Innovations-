Carrot Cultivation Guide

Carrot (Daucus carota) is a cool-season root vegetable grown for its sweet and nutritious taproot. It grows best in cool to moderately warm climates, with well-drained sandy loam soil rich in organic matter. Carrots require consistent moisture and loose soil to form smooth, uniform roots.

Climate Requirements

Temperature: Ideal growing temperature is 15–25°C. Roots become woody and bitter above 30°C.

Daylight: Carrots need 6–8 hours of sunlight daily for proper root development.

Humidity: Moderate humidity (60–70%) is suitable. Excess humidity may cause fungal diseases.

Soil Requirements

Carrots thrive in deep, loose, sandy loam or loamy soils with high organic content. Avoid stony or compact soils, as they cause forked or deformed roots. Maintain soil pH between 6.0 and 6.8 for best results.

Watering

Carrots require regular and uniform moisture for root enlargement. Irrigate lightly but frequently (once every 3–4 days in dry conditions). Avoid waterlogging, which can cause root rot. Drip irrigation is recommended for even soil moisture.

Harvesting

Carrots are ready for harvest in 70–100 days depending on the variety. Harvest when roots are 2–3 cm in diameter at the crown. Gently loosen the soil before pulling to avoid breaking the roots.
