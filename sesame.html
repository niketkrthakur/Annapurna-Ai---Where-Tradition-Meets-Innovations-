Sesame Plant

Sesame Cultivation Guide

Sesame (Sesamum indicum), also known as Til, is one of the oldest oilseed crops in the world. It is valued for its high oil content, nutritional value, and drought resistance. India is one of the largest producers of sesame, mainly cultivated in rainfed conditions.

Climate Requirements

Temperature: Optimal range is 25–35°C. Sensitive to frost and waterlogging.

Rainfall: Requires 500–650 mm rainfall during growth. Excess rain during flowering reduces yield.

Season: Grown in both Kharif (monsoon) and Rabi (post-monsoon) seasons depending on region.

Soil Requirements

Sesame grows well in well-drained sandy loam soils. Ideal soil pH is 6.0–7.5. Heavy clay or saline soils should be avoided.

Watering

Sesame is mainly a rainfed crop but benefits from 2–3 protective irrigations, especially during flowering and pod formation. Avoid excessive watering to prevent diseases.

Harvesting

Sesame crop is ready in 90–120 days after sowing. Harvest when lower leaves start yellowing and pods begin to turn brown. Plants are cut, stacked, and sun-dried before threshing to collect seeds.
