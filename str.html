Fresh Strawberry Plants

Strawberry Cultivation Guide

Strawberries grow best in a temperate climate with temperatures between 13–22°C (55–72°F), needing 8–12 hours of daylight for flowering and fruiting. They thrive in well-draining, sandy loam or loam soil with a slightly acidic pH of 5.7–6.5, enriched with compost or aged manure. Consistent moisture is crucial, as strawberry plants have shallow roots and are not drought-tolerant, but overwatering must be avoided to prevent root rot.

Climate and Light

Temperature: 13–22°C (55–72°F) is ideal. Temperatures above 25°C (77°F) negatively affect fruit set.

Photoperiod (Daylight): Short-day plants; need 8–12 hours of daylight for about 10 days to form flower buds.

Humidity: Ideal around 60%. Ensure good ventilation to prevent mold and disease.

Soil Requirements

Strawberries prefer well-draining sandy loam or loam soil with high organic matter content. Maintain soil pH between 5.7 and 6.5. Add compost or aged manure before planting.

Watering

Consistent moisture is essential due to shallow roots. Avoid waterlogging to prevent root rot. Drip irrigation is recommended for even water distribution.
