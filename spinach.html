Spinach Cultivation Guide

Spinach is a cool-season leafy vegetable that thrives in mild climates. Optimal temperatures are Daytime 15–20°C (59–68°F), Nighttime 8–15°C (46–59°F). It can tolerate light frost but bolts quickly in hot weather. Spinach prefers well-draining loamy or sandy loam soil rich in organic matter, with a pH of 6.0–7.5. Regular watering is essential to keep the soil moist and ensure tender leaves, but waterlogging should be avoided.

Climate and Light

Temperature: Ideal range is 15–20°C (59–68°F). Growth slows below 5°C (41°F) and plants bolt above 25°C (77°F).

Photoperiod (Daylight): Spinach grows best with 6–8 hours of sunlight daily, though it can tolerate partial shade.

Humidity: Moderate humidity (50–60%) is ideal. Very high humidity may encourage downy mildew and leaf spot diseases.

Soil Requirements

Spinach grows well in loamy or sandy loam soils that are fertile and well-drained. Maintain soil pH between 6.0 and 7.5. Add compost or well-rotted manure before planting for better yields. Avoid heavy clay soils that retain too much moisture.

Watering

Spinach requires consistent and light watering to keep the soil evenly moist. Water every 2–3 days in dry conditions to prevent leaves from becoming bitter. Overwatering should be avoided as it may cause root rot and fungal diseases. Drip irrigation or gentle sprinkling is recommended for leafy crops.
