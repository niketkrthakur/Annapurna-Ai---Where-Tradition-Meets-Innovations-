Sapota (Chikoo) Cultivation Guide

Sapota, also known as Chikoo, is a popular tropical fruit tree cultivated for its sweet, brown, pulpy fruit. It is grown mainly in warm climates and is valued for fresh consumption as well as for making jams, milkshakes, and desserts.

Climate Requirements

Temperature: Best growth occurs at 25–35°C. The tree cannot tolerate severe frost.

Rainfall: Requires 125–250 cm annually, but waterlogging must be avoided.

Humidity: Moderate to high humidity is ideal for fruit set and quality.

Soil Requirements

Sapota thrives in a wide range of soils including alluvial, sandy loam, and black soils. However, well-drained, deep soils rich in organic matter are best. The ideal soil pH is 6.5 to 8.0.

Watering

Young sapota trees require regular watering during establishment. Mature trees are relatively drought-tolerant but benefit from irrigation during dry months for better yields. Avoid standing water around the roots.

Harvesting

Sapota trees start bearing fruit after 3–4 years (grafted) or 6–7 years (seedling). The fruits are harvested when they turn brown and soft to touch. Proper harvesting at maturity ensures sweetness and shelf life.
