Soybean Cultivation Guide

Soybean is an important oilseed and protein-rich legume crop. It grows best in warm, moderately humid climates and requires fertile soils with good drainage. Being a legume crop, it also helps in fixing atmospheric nitrogen, enriching the soil for subsequent crops.

Climate Requirements

Temperature: Best growth occurs between 20–30°C. Temperatures below 15°C or above 40°C reduce yield.

Rainfall: Requires 500–700 mm well-distributed rainfall during the growing season.

Sunlight: Needs 8–10 hours of sunlight daily for good pod formation and seed development.

Soil Requirements

Soybean grows well in well-drained loamy or clay loam soils rich in organic matter. Soil pH should be maintained between 6.0 and 7.5. Avoid waterlogged soils, as standing water leads to root and stem rot.

Watering

Soybean requires moderate watering. Irrigate at critical growth stages such as flowering, pod formation, and seed filling. Excess irrigation or waterlogging should be avoided to prevent diseases.

Harvesting

Soybeans are ready for harvest when the leaves turn yellow and fall, and pods become dry and brown. Harvesting is usually done after 90–120 days depending on the variety. Timely harvest prevents seed shattering and quality loss.
